ⓘ Methodology & Data Sources

What this shows

Every one of the 48 teams at the 2026 World Cup must travel between a training camp and three group-stage venues spread across the USA, Canada and Mexico. This tool maps those journeys and estimates the distance, carbon emissions and jet-lag burden each team faces.

Distance

Distances are great-circle (straight-line "as the crow flies") between city coordinates, calculated with the haversine formula. Each team's total is the sum of round trips from their camp to each of their three group venues and back (camp → game → camp, ×3). Real flight paths are slightly longer, so these are conservative lower-bound figures.

Carbon emissions (tCO₂e)

Emissions use the UK Government (DESNZ) 2025 conversion factors, banded by leg distance — the same methodology as the fan-travel figures — rather than a single flat flight factor. Each leg's per-passenger factor (all including radiative forcing, the extra warming at altitude) is: short domestic ≤1,000 km 0.22928, short-haul <3,700 km 0.12786, and long-haul ≥3,700 km 0.15282 kgCO₂e/pax-km. We assume a travelling party of 45 people, so a leg = 45 × its banded factor × km. Group legs are camp→game→camp round trips; knockout legs are one-way city-to-city hops. Figures are shown in tonnes (tCO₂e).

For the Qatar 2022 comparison, journeys were by coach, so we use the DESNZ bus factor of 0.02776 kgCO₂e per passenger-km instead.

Jet lag & "disadvantage score"

Timezone shifts are the difference in UTC offset between a team's camp and each venue. The disadvantage score sums the timezone hours crossed before each game, divided by the days of rest before that game — so crossing 2 zones with 3 days' recovery scores worse than the same shift with 7 days. It's a relative indicator, not a medical measure.

Fairness Index

For each group: (furthest-travelling team's km − least-travelling team's km) ÷ group average × 100. A score of 0 means all four teams travel equally; higher means a bigger gap.

Live standings

Match results are entered manually as games finish and sorted by the official FIFA tiebreakers (points, then goal difference, then goals scored). Standings, the bracket and the fairness figures update from these results.

Qualification & title predictions

The "Q%" and title odds come from a Monte Carlo simulation: the tournament is played out thousands of times in your browser. Matches still to come are decided by a probability model based on team strength ratings, with results already entered held fixed. Each team's percentage is simply how often it qualified (or won) across all those simulated tournaments. The numbers re-compute automatically whenever a new result is added, which is why they shift as the group stage unfolds. They're modelled estimates, not predictions of any single outcome.

Altitude exposure

Host cities range from sea level to Mexico City at ~2,240m. At altitude there's less oxygen, so teams not acclimatised tire faster and the ball travels further through thinner air. We measure each team's "exposure" as the climb from its training base to its match venues — only upward jumps count, and the effect is weighted to reflect that altitude barely matters below ~1,000m but rises steeply above ~1,500m. A team based high (e.g. in Mexico) faces no shock playing at altitude, mirroring how acclimatisation works in reality. It's an exposure indicator, not a performance prediction.

Knockout-stage travel

Once the group stage ends, knockout travel is modelled differently. Teams no longer return to a fixed base between games — they tour onward from city to city — so knockout legs are one-way hops (last group venue → Round-of-32 city → Round-of-16 city → …), not round trips. Each team's path follows the official bracket. A leg shows as projected until its fixture is played, then converts to actual as results are entered. The map only draws knockout flights once a game has genuinely been played.

Fan travel (estimate)

The Fan Travel tab is a separate, deliberately rough estimate of supporter travel — not the precise, fixture-based figures used everywhere else. Spectator travel dwarfs team travel (independent estimates put fans at the overwhelming majority of a World Cup's transport footprint), so it's worth showing, but it depends on assumptions that can't be known precisely in advance. Treat it as an order-of-magnitude indicator, not a measurement.

For each nation we estimate a travelling-fan count and model those fans as one cohort that follows the team on a one-way tour — home nation → each group venue → each knockout venue — never returning home between games, mirroring how the team itself is modelled. Per-fan distances are great-circle, scaled by the estimated fan count.

Emissions use the same UK DESNZ 2025 factors, but banded by leg distance rather than a single flight factor: coach for hops ≤300 km (0.02776), short domestic ≤1,000 km (0.22928), short-haul <3,700 km (0.12786) and long-haul ≥3,700 km (0.15282) kgCO₂e per passenger-km, all including radiative forcing. The long inbound leg from the home nation is typically the bulk of the total; hops between North American venues are comparatively small. Each team's figure is split into international inbound and North-America-domestic travel, which reveals that roughly a fifth of fan emissions are generated within the host continent once supporters arrive.

Group-stage values are fixed; knockout legs are added live as the bracket resolves, shown as projected until a game is played and actual afterwards. Fan-count estimates reflect real-world 2026 conditions (US entry and visa friction, FIFA ticket-purchase data) and are necessarily approximate — the headline is the scale and shape of fan travel, not any single nation's exact tonnage.

UK TV coverage (BBC / ITV)

The Fixtures tab shows whether each group-stage match airs on the BBC or ITV in the UK — the two free-to-air broadcasters share the tournament and split the games. Channel labels (e.g. BBC One, ITV1, ITV4) follow the broadcasters' published selections. Knockout-round channels are assigned closer to each round and are added once confirmed. All UK kick-off times are shown in BST.

Sources & caveats

Fixtures, venues and training-camp locations from FIFA's published 2026 schedule. Emission factors from UK DESNZ 2025. UK broadcaster (BBC/ITV) assignments from the channels' published selections. Fan-travel figures are independent estimates. This is an independent analysis for illustration — actual team logistics (charter routing, exact squad sizes, road vs air choices) will vary. Treat the numbers as well-grounded estimates, not official figures.
